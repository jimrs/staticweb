Harmony

No composer in the history has ever avoided the rigorous study of the rules of harmony, and it is the most salient part of so called primitive music, e.g. pop.

The art of joining chords

The roman numeral system is used to simplify the analysis of a musical composition. It makes the chords independent from a tonal system, giving each of them a function. This function is the same in every key. For example, in the key of C major the C major harmony is given the tonic function I, the G major harmony is given the dominant function V. In G major, the C major harmony is given the subdominant function of IV, while the G major harmony is given the tonic function I.

The most important harmonic functions are, on the highest level of abstraction, tension and release, or movement and calm. The tonic function represents a release, calm. The dominant function gives the strongest feeling of tension. The reason for this is the third of a dominant chord. It is a semitone lower than the tonic tone and moves strongly to the tonic.
