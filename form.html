Form

Music is the most abstract form of language, and every language needs proper punctuation.

The art of punctuation

Without the knowledge of form, music is just incoherent mass of sound. Imagine talking to someone who first mentions how he likes to jump on a trampoline and then immediately moves on to talk about the voltage of light bulbs. Form teaches us to organize sound, to divide it into smaller parts, to connect these parts, to develop motives, and to give meaning to sound.

No aspiring composer is able to envision in his head the whole composition as a beginner. It is important to start with small forms and move up to the big forms. The smallest unit is a motive, then a phrase, a sentence (or a period), a double period, binary form...
